4. ANALYSIS: WHY EVIDENTIAL EDL MSE LOSS FAILED (Attempts 1–3)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  The Sensoy 2018 evidential MSE loss was designed for large datasets
  (MNIST: 60,000 samples; CIFAR-10: 50,000 samples). In a 5-way 5-shot
  episode we have only 25 training samples. This creates two problems:

  A) KL regularization is too aggressive
     The KL term KL[Dir(alpha) || Dir(1)] penalises any non-uniform
     alpha, pushing the model towards maximum uncertainty (alpha → 1).
     On 60K samples this is mild regularization. On 25 samples it
     overwhelms the MSE signal. Even at kl_weight=0.1, the model gets
     stuck at 60% support accuracy.

  B) The var_term (p*(1-p)/(S+1)) in the MSE loss acts as implicit
     regularization even without KL. It adds a penalty for ALL classes
     (not just wrong ones), slowing convergence on tiny support sets.

  C) ReLU activation causes dead neurons
     When head logits are negative (half the time at init), relu outputs
     zero → zero gradient through that path → model can't recover.
     Switching to softplus (smooth approximation of relu, always > 0)
     fixes this but the MSE loss itself was still the bottleneck.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. ANALYSIS: WHY CE TRAINING WITH DIRICHLET EVALUATION WORKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Replacing evidential MSE with cross-entropy during training fixes the
  accuracy issue because CE gradients are much sharper: the gradient of
  CE for the correct class is (p_correct - 1), which is large and clear
  when the model is wrong.

  The Dirichlet output (softplus evidence) is still principled at test time:
    - For in-distribution inputs: model is confident → softplus(logits)
      large → S = sum(alpha) large → vacuity K/S small
    - For OOD inputs (SVHN): model has never seen digit-style features →
      softplus(logits) smaller → S smaller → vacuity K/S larger
  This gap in vacuity is why OOD AUROC jumps from ~0.70 to 0.991.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. ANALYSIS: WHY EVIDENTIAL ECE IS BAD (0.526) DESPITE GOOD ACCURACY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  This is the most surprising result and needs careful explanation.

  After CE training to near-zero loss:
    - The head logits for the correct class become very large
    - softplus(large_logit) → very large evidence
    - S = sum(alpha) = sum(softplus(logits) + 1) becomes very large
    - p_correct = alpha_correct / S → approaches 1.0 (near-certain)

  So for 75 query images:
    - The model says it is ~98% confident on most predictions
    - But actual accuracy is 89.3%
    - This overconfidence gap → high ECE (0.526)

  For softmax:
    - softmax(logits) has a built-in normalizing denominator that bounds
      max probability more naturally
    - Competition between classes prevents extreme overconfidence
    - This is why softmax ECE = 0.040 (nearly perfectly calibrated)

  Root cause: CE training makes BOTH models overconfident on support set,
  but softmax normalisation naturally limits the maximum confidence,
  while Dirichlet's S grows unbounded → pathological overconfidence on
  in-distribution samples.

  Possible fixes (future work):
    - Temperature scaling on logits before computing softplus
    - Label smoothing during training
    - Use EDL-MSE loss with very small kl_weight (0.001) on more data
    - Proper episodic meta-training over many episodes (not single episode)

7. THE THESIS STORY (what this demo proves and does NOT prove)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  WHAT IS PROVEN:
    ✓ RQ3 (PARTIAL): Evidential Dirichlet head dramatically improves OOD
      detection vs Softmax in a low-data regime (5-shot):
      AUROC 0.991 vs 0.854 (+0.137) — a very strong result.
    ✓ Both models achieve similar accuracy (~89–93%) with only ~20K
      trainable params (0.17% of ResNet18). PEFT is effective.

  WHAT IS NOT PROVEN IN THIS DEMO:
    ✗ RQ2: Evidential does NOT show better calibration (ECE) than Softmax
      in this single-episode demo. ECE is 0.526 vs 0.040 (softmax wins).
    ~ This is a known limitation of using EDL in the few-shot regime
      without proper multi-episode meta-training.

  THE HONEST SLIDE NARRATIVE:
    "With the same 20K-parameter adapter, the evidential head provides
     dramatically better OOD detection (AUROC +0.137) at near-equal
     accuracy. Calibration improvement requires meta-training over many
     episodes — this is the main open question for the full thesis."

  2026-04-21: Switched training loss from evidential MSE to cross-entropy

              MSE signal on 25 support samples. CE gives stable training
              while Dirichlet probs at test time still give principled OOD.

              Reason: relu causes dead neurons (zero gradient for negative
              logits). Softplus is always smooth and positive.

              Reason: Even kl_weight=0.1 prevents convergence on 25 samples.

  2026-04-21: lr=5e-3 (up from original 1e-3). Reason: faster convergence.
              num_steps=300 (up from 200). Reason: more headroom for convergence.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. WHAT TO FIX FOR THE FULL THESIS (after pre-defence)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Proper episodic meta-training: sample N episodes per epoch, aggregate
     gradients. This trains the model to generalise across episodes, which
     should fix the ECE problem (model won't overfit a single support set).

  2. Proper EDL-MSE training at scale: with many episodes, the KL term
     becomes reasonable and the model can be trained end-to-end with the
     Sensoy loss as originally intended.

  3. Temperature scaling: simple post-hoc calibration on a validation set.
     Would likely fix the ECE without any retraining.

  4. LoRA, BitFit: replace the bottleneck adapter with other PEFT methods.
     Compare calibration and OOD across adapter types.

  5. More backbones and datasets: MobileNetV3-Small, ConvNeXt-Nano on
     CUB-200 and ISIC (medical imaging).
